CHAPTER 2. PATHOLOGY OF LYING: CASCADE OF DESTRUCTIVE ARCHETYPES

2.1. Cascade activation model (according to DVOICH)  

A lie is not an isolated act. It triggers a chain reaction. Stages:

1. Initiation (Pheasant). Primary lie, embellishment, concealment of an error. The goal – to avoid immediate punishment.
2. Intensification (Bore). Creation of complex, tangled justifications to legitimise the lie. The more words, the harder it is to get to the truth.
3. Paranoia (Draft). Fear of exposure. A person begins to see threats in every question, check, glance. Constant stress.
4. Chronification (Mole). Lying becomes a habit. A person lies automatically, even without benefit. This is his “comfort zone”.
5. Aggression (Eggplant). When the lie begins to crumble under the pressure of facts, brute force is activated – threats, pressure, attempts to suppress the opponent.
6. Collapse (Dummy / Kissel). Exhaustion of all resources. Apathy, imitation of activity, withdrawal into “spirituality” as a justification for inaction.

Stopping the cascade: a return to #BT at any stage. The earlier, the cheaper.
